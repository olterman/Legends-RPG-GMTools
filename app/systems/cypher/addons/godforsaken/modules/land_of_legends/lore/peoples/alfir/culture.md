# Alfir Culture

Alfir culture is rooted in memory, refinement, and fracture.

They are immortal, but not unified. Shared origin gives them common gravity, yet each major branch expresses Alfir identity differently.

The Kalaquendi preserve scholarship, diplomacy, sea-travel, astronomy, and open exchange. The Galadhrim treat culture as stewardship of the living world rather than city-building. The Faltrim carry memory through migration, resonance, and the songs of leviathans. The Duathrim translate culture into discipline, secrecy, caste, and function.

What binds them is not agreement, but a shared inheritance:

- a lost world that still haunts collective memory
- the burden of being first arrivals and unwilling world-changers
- long memory that makes grief and pride equally durable
- a tendency to frame survival as duty rather than improvisation

To outsiders, the Alfir may appear ancient and singular. In truth they are a family of related civilizations, all still answering the question of what exile made them.
