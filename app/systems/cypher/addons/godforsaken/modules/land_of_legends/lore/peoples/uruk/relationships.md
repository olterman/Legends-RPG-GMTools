# Uruk Relationships

Uruk relationships are shaped by proximity, conflict, and misunderstood depth.

Highland Uruk maintain a wary but durable coexistence with Fenmir Highlanders based on shared harshness, oath culture, and mutual respect. Lowland Uruk more often stand in adversarial relationship to the settled world, especially where conquest and territorial pressure define their politics.

Other peoples tend to see only the war-band image, but Uruk relationships are more varied:

- Highland Uruk relate to land, herd, and poem as much as to battle
- Lowland Uruk relate to war through communal identity and the Red Surge of Gor-Kha
- both branches carry oral memory of migration and lost origin

The crucial setting truth is that the Uruk are not aberrant intruders without culture. They are a people whose relationships to others are intensified by how strongly the world fears them.
