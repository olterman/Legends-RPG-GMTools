# **Daelgast,** Gulgoroth
_The Horror-Void-Beyond-the-World_
Daelgast is not a people.
It is not a god, nor a demon, nor a will that can be reasoned with.
Daelgast is **what came through with the Alfirin**.
---
## The Thing Between
When the Alfirin fled their dying world, they did not cross empty distance. Between worlds lies the **interstitial aether**—a realm of unformed resonance, discarded possibility, and unrealized endings.
The Velim knew this place.
It was not meant to be touched.
The Gate that was opened was meant to be **brief**, **narrow**, and **self-closing**. But the Alfirin passage was vast, sustained, and reinforced. In forcing continuity, something else adhered to the crossing.
Not an entity.
A **condition**.
Daelgast was not invited.
It **clung**.
---
## Arrival Without Intention
Daelgast did not enter the world as an invader.
It **precipitated**.
Like rot forming around a splinter, Daelgast condensed where alien resonance met damaged reality. It was not alive—but it behaved as corruption behaves: spreading along stress-lines, pooling where flow was disrupted.
It had no purpose.
But it had **compatibility**.
---
## The Unresolved Wound
Daelgast persists because the Gate was never healed.
Reality did not close behind the Alfirin; it calcified around them. Immortality, permanence, and anchored light created regions where cycles failed to complete.
Daelgast thrives only where:
- Death does not fully release
- Magic stagnates instead of flowing
- Time hesitates
- Endings are denied
It is not fed by malice.
It is fed by **unresolved passage**.
---
## Undeath as Symptom
Undead are not creations of Daelgast.
They are **diagnoses**.
When a soul cannot depart cleanly, when memory lingers without breath, Daelgast occupies the mismatch. Flesh moves again not through command, but through **echo**—habit without life.
More grotesque manifestations occur when strong identity, prolonged suffering, or concentrated magic provide a deeper substrate for corruption.
These beings do not serve Daelgast.
They are **expressions of contamination**.
---
## Anti-Beauty
Daelgast is anathema to life not because it hates beauty—but because beauty requires resolution.
In Daelgast-tainted places:
- Colors dull or over-saturate unnaturally
- Symmetry fails
- Sound deadens
- Growth twists into repetition
- Magic becomes heavy and tired
It does not destroy.
It **spoils**.
---
## Why It Cannot Be Destroyed
Daelgast cannot be slain, exorcised, or reasoned with.
Because it is not _there_.
It is the shadow cast by a decision still upheld.
As long as:
- The Gate remains unhealed
- The Alfirin remain anchored by immortality
- Reality is forced to remember a crossing that should have ended
…Daelgast will continue to manifest.
Not everywhere.
Only where the world is weakest.
---
## The Velim Position
The Velim do not speak of Daelgast as evil.
They call it **load**.
They seal, isolate, and contain—not to defeat it, but to prevent spread. To them, Daelgast is proof that some passages should never be stabilized.
A wound can scar.
A wound left open **infects**.
---
## The Alfirin Silence
Alfirin public histories do not name Daelgast.
Older orders do.
In private.
They fear it not because it threatens them—but because it is **bound to their survival**.
To heal the Gate fully might mean relinquishing the permanence that saved them.
And so the world bears the cost.
---
## A Galadhrim Saying
> _“The forest knows when something never died.”_

---
## What Daelgast Means for the World
Daelgast is not the end of all things.
It is the reminder that something _ended incorrectly_.
And until that is answered……the Horror-Void-Beyond-the-World will remain.
---
# The Deepening of Daelgast
_The World After the Second Scar_
---
## The Recent Worsening: **The Sundering of Cael-Tharûn**
Less than a century ago, an Alfirin enclave known as **Cael-Tharûn** attempted what its scholars called a _Corrective Anchoring_.
Publicly, it was framed as a stabilizing ritual—an effort to reinforce weakened ley convergence near an old Gate-scar. Privately, its true purpose was more dangerous: to **partially reopen the original passage** in a controlled manner, allowing accumulated strain to “vent” back into the interstitial aether.
The Velim warned against it.
They were ignored.
---
### What Went Wrong
The ritual succeeded—_briefly_.
For a single night, the Gate-scar thinned. Light bent. Time slowed. The Alfirin felt something ease.
But the passage did not vent - It **reconnected**.
For moments measured in heartbeats, the world was once again exposed to the space between worlds—and Daelgast, which had never truly left that place, responded.
Not by entering…
…but by **thickening**.
When the ritual collapsed:
- Leylines inverted or stagnated
- Death failed repeatedly in the surrounding region
- Corpses rose without coherence
- Magic became brittle and dangerous
Cael-Tharûn vanished beneath a spreading zone of corruption now called **the Pall of Tharûn**.
---
## The Second Scar
Velim refer to this event as **the Second Scar**.
Unlike the first, which stretched across the world in subtle ways, the Second Scar is localized, intense, and actively expanding.
It proves what the Velim had long feared:
Daelgast does not diminish when addressed improperly. It **adapts to containment**.
---
# The Cult of the Hollow Accord
_Those Who Mistake Absence for Truth_
---
## Who They Are
The **Hollow Accord** is a loose, decentralized movement that has arisen in the shadow of the Pall of Tharûn and similar afflicted regions.
They do not call Daelgast a god.
They call it **Revelation**.
Members are drawn from many peoples:
- Broken veterans
- Failed mages
- Exiled scholars
- Those who have survived undeath, possession, or corruption and lived
They believe they have seen the world **without pretense**.
---
## Their Belief (And Their Error)
The Hollow Accord teaches that:
- Life is a lie sustained by forced order
- Beauty is an artificial constraint
- The Alfirin error proves permanence is a mistake
- Daelgast is the _natural state_ of existence revealed
They believe exposure brings clarity.
They believe corruption strips away illusion.
They are wrong.
Daelgast does not enlighten.
It **erodes**.
But erosion feels honest to those tired of holding themselves together.
---
## Rituals and Practices
The Accord does not summon Daelgast.
They **prepare themselves** for it.
Their rites include:
- Refusing burial
- Prolonged vigil in tainted zones
- Self-exposure to necrotic resonance
- Deliberate fracturing of identity and memory
Some members gain unsettling resilience or distorted magical effects.
Most simply decay faster.
---
## Why They Are Dangerous
The Hollow Accord spreads Daelgast unintentionally.
They build sanctuaries in contaminated zones, refuse containment, sabotage Velim seals, and oppose Galadhrim cleansing efforts.
They believe they are midwives of a truer world.
In reality, they are **opening doors that cannot close**.
---
## How Other Peoples Respond
**Velim** treat the Accord as a containment breach
**Galadhrim** destroy their sites without negotiation
**Alfirin** quietly hunt their leaders while denying their significance
**Humans** mistake them for demon cultists—and underestimate them
**Fellic** avoid their paths entirely
---
## A Warning Recovered from Tharûn
Carved into stone, author unknown:
> _“It does not answer._
> _It does not watch._
> _It only remains where it should not.”_

---
## Why This Matters Now
The Second Scar proves the world is no longer merely enduring Daelgast.
It is **accumulating it**.
And for the first time, there are those who believe this is not a tragedy — but a calling.
