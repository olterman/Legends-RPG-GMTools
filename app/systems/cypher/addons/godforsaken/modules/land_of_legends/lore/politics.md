# Politics

Politics in the Lands of Legends is rarely about simple kingship or territorial control. It is about how peoples respond to fracture, memory, scarcity, and dangerous power.

Several recurring political patterns define the setting:

- immortal elites and councils that can plan in centuries rather than years
- trade states and coastal powers that convert flexibility into influence
- theological regimes that weaponize principle into law
- hidden orders that prefer infiltration over open rule
- tribal and oral systems that preserve legitimacy through continuity rather than bureaucracy

This makes politics inseparable from worldview. Caldoran power is tied to interpretation, law, and mercantile reach. Xanthir power is tied to absolutist resistance that can harden into fanaticism. Duathrim power depends on secrecy, cells, and patient manipulation. Fenmir highlanders and other ancestral cultures ground authority in memory, custom, and relationship to land rather than centralized statecraft.

The central tension is that no political system in the setting is neutral. Every one of them is also a philosophy of survival.
