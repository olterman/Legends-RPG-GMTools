# Fellic History

Fellic history is remembered as continuity rather than arrival.

Unlike the Alfirin, the Uruk, or humankind, the Fellic do not define themselves through migration myths. Their oldest stories insist they were already part of the world before the great known upheavals, listening within the First Silence before the bright wound in the sky changed everything.

Their myths remember the appearance of that wound, the intervention of the Velim, the coming of the Alfirin, and the Pact of Fur and Leaf with the Galadhrim. In Fellic memory, these are not separate folklore fragments. They are the turning points by which the world stopped being effortless and had to be watched more carefully.

This makes Fellic history feel pre-imperial, ecological, and mythically observant. They are not historians of kings. They are historians of shifts in the world's breathing.
