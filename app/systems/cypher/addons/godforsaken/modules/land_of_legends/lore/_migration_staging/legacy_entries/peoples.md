# Peoples Overview
# Races of the Lands of Legends — Quick Reference
## [[The Alfirin Tribes]]
Immortal exiles from a shattered world; entered through damaged Gates after failed negotiation with the Velim; fractured into Sky Children (open, cultured), Forest Folk (Galadhrim, wild stewards), and Nightshades (Duathrim, secretive manipulators).
## [[The Small Folk]] - Velim
Tiny, luminous custodians of Gates and reality; existed before the Alfirin arrival; forced into guardianship after the passage went wrong; neutral, ancient, and quietly essential.
## [[The Human Tribes]] (Fenmir, Caldorans, Xanthir, Free Cities)
Mortal, adaptable, and diverse; Fenmir are proud, spiritual highlanders and freebooting lowlanders; Caldorans build trade empires; Xanthir follow a militant theocracy; scattered tribes survive in wetlands and sands.
## [[The Uruk Tribes]]
Powerful, nomadic and tribal warriors who arrived suddenly from the east; Highland Uruk are philosophical herders and poets; Lowland Uruk are militant conquerors, shaping their feared reputation. (orc-like lets not use the word orc) larger than human. brotruding lower tusks, highland uruk blue skinned, lowland uruk green skinned.
## [[The Lilim]]
Cosmic children of rebellion and innovation; inherently magical wandering performers and thinkers; no homeland or state, bound by inner calling and shared ritual language (Darb-Naṣr). As children of lilith they have thiefling traits but lets not use the word thiefling)
## [[The Fellic]]
Ancient beast-aspected peoples who have always lived in the wild; observers and balancers tied to Galadhrim and Velim; divided into Cat (watchers), Fox (weavers), Dog (guardians), Bear (elders), bull (keepers of the land) and turtle (time guardians), mallard (keepers of trade and commerce friends of humans)
## [[The Small Folk]] - Gitz
Small, ingenious forest-dwellers of the Fenmir wilds; adaptive survivors with oral histories that predate Alfirin settlement; reject Uruk kinship myths. green skinned (lets not use the word goblin)
## [[The Small Folk]] - Vaettyr
Brilliant subterranean tinkerfolk; maintain ancient machines that stabilize magic and reality; claim awakening due to damage caused by the Alfirin passage. (gnome-like lets not use the word gnome)
## [[The Duergar]]
Deep-dwelling, austere and insular folk of stone and shadow; master miners, engineers, and forge-clans; culturally isolationist with grudging respect for ancient pacts. (Dwarf-like lets not use the word dwarf)
