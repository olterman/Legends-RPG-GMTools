# Descriptor Culture

The ancestral descriptors are cultural compression tools.

Each one takes a people, lineage, or subgroup and translates its pressures into playable emphasis: what that background rewards, what it hinders, and what kind of dramatic instinct it should place in a character's hands.

That means the descriptor set should preserve Land of Legends distinctions rather than flatten them into generic fantasy races:

- Kalaquendi should feel open, high-minded, and horizon-shaped
- Duathrim should feel disciplined, concealed, and strategic
- Highland Uruk should feel thoughtful, enduring, and severe
- Lowland Uruk should feel forceful, practical, and conquest-shaped
- Fenmir should split meaningfully between highland memory and lowland/free-city adaptation
- Lilim should feel mobile, expressive, and anti-static
- Duergar should feel weighted, deliberate, and duty-bound

When the descriptors work well, they make setting identity immediately playable without reducing the setting to clichés.
