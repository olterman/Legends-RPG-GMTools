# Caldor Island Overview

Caldor Island is one of the great contradictions of the western sea: a land claimed on charts and in law, but never fully known in practice.

Its coasts support trade, fishing, and small communities tied to the wider Caldoran world, but the interior remains broken by mountain walls, bad weather, false roads, and valleys that seem to belong to an older rhythm of life. Official maps speak with confidence the island does not deserve.

For play, Caldor Island works best as a frontier threshold:

- close enough to civilization to matter
- remote enough to hide forgotten truths
- structured enough for settlements to feel grounded
- wild enough for mystery, danger, and discovery

The island is also a useful starting frame because it contains both edges of the setting at once: the social familiarity of villages and trade routes, and the deeper uncertainty of isolated passes, hidden basins, and places the maps never really reached.
