# The Others Relationships

The peoples grouped as the Others are defined by the way they disturb ordinary categories.

The Lilim unsettle power because they move, transform, perform, and catalyze change without building states. The Velim unsettle power because they maintain boundaries and systems others barely perceive. Both are hard to incorporate into imperial, national, or doctrinal frameworks.

Their relationships with the settled world therefore tend toward fear, fascination, utility, and misinterpretation. They are welcomed temporarily, condemned loudly, or relied upon secretly.

What unites them is not common origin. It is the way the world keeps trying, and failing, to classify them safely.
