# Relationships

The Lands of Legends is held together by unstable relationships rather than universal order.

The most important of these relationships are structural:

- Daelgast is the wound
- Ul-Nha'rath is the belief that the wound should remain open
- Lilith embodies motion and refusal
- the Red God embodies resistance and sacrifice
- the Breath of the World exists only where the living continue to choose presence
- the Old Gods persist as distant resonances, no longer fully aligned with reality

Peoples and cultures are defined by how they stand among these forces and among one another.

The Alfir remain bound by origin but divided by purpose. The Duergar and Velim relate to the world through stabilization and containment. Human cultures are plural, often contradictory, and deeply responsive to geography. The Uruk are split between endurance and combustion. The Lilim move through the world rather than ruling it. Fellic and other elder peoples serve as living memory for rhythms others have forgotten.

No single culture has the whole truth. The setting works because relationships are partial, tense, and often mutually misread.
