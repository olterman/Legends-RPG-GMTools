# Caldor Culture

Caldor culture is urban, ambitious, mercantile, and self-consciously progressive.

Its cities bind law, trade, scholarship, engineering, theology, and artistic display into one cultural project: the belief that the world can be improved through structured effort. Merchant guilds, workshops, universities, canals, shipyards, and salons all participate in that same civic imagination.

This gives Caldor real brilliance. It also creates pressure. Progress can become moral vanity, and openness can become a softer form of empire. Caldor should feel genuinely generative and genuinely dangerous for exactly the same reasons.
