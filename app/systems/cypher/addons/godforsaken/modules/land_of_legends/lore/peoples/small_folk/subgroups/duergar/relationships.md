# Duergar Relationships

Duergar relationships with the surface world are governed by trust scarcity and contract clarity.

They trade reluctantly, prefer enforceable obligations over goodwill, and evaluate other peoples through endurance, memory, and reliability rather than warmth. They respect the Fenmir for oath-keeping, acknowledge the Alfirin for ancient memory while distrusting their detachment, and see the Uruk as powerful but dangerously uncontrolled.

Their strongest cultural distrust is reserved for the Lilim. This is not because the Lilim are simply dangerous, but because they embody what Duergar fear most in social form: motion without anchor, change without structural accounting, and meaning unbound from fixed duty.

That tension is useful in the setting. Duergar and Lilim are ideological opposites even when neither side is wholly wrong.
