# Lilim Relationships

The settled world rarely knows what to do with the Lilim.

They are feared as demonic wanderers, desired as performers and lovers, condemned as heretics and agitators, and quietly sought out wherever old orders need to be loosened. Churches denounce them, empires watch them, and revolutions often begin where they linger too long.

This tension is central to their place in the setting. The Lilim do not usually seek dominion, but they leave altered societies behind them. Their presence erodes certainty, unsettles hierarchy, and gives the oppressed language for change.

To the Duergar, they are distrusted because they do not anchor themselves. To more rigid societies, they are dangerous because they embody movement and ambiguity. To those trapped in dead structures, they can look like liberation.
