# Small Folk Culture

The Small Folk are ancient, clever, and structurally important in ways larger peoples routinely fail to understand.

They thrive in overlooked spaces: forest edges, tunnels, workshops, seams in reality, forgotten machinery, and thresholds between systems. Their cultures do not depend on size, visibility, or imperial presence. They depend on persistence.

Within that broader identity:

- Gitz culture values adaptability, clan skill, trapcraft, and improvisation
- Vaettyr culture values invention, apprenticeship, and subterranean experimentation
- Velim existence is tied to threshold, containment, and the maintenance of reality itself
- Duergar, now grouped with Small Folk in the rebuild taxonomy, embody endurance, pressure, and deep responsibility

Small Folk culture is best understood not as minor civilization, but as distributed guardianship.
