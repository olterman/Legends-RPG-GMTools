# Uruk Overview

To the lowlands and frontier songs, the Uruk are too often treated as monsters given flesh: raiders, warbands, tusks, and iron. The truth is older and more complex.

Uruk memory speaks of an eastern homeland now lost, of migrations carried by hunger, prophecy, or forgotten disaster. Whatever their origin, they did not arrive as a single culture. Their branches diverged under different pressures and produced different social shapes.

In Land of Legends, the key distinction is between:

- Highland Uruk, shaped by harsh uplands, wary coexistence, philosophy, and oral tradition
- Lowland Uruk, shaped by conquest, pressure, contested plains, and militant survival

The Uruk are therefore not a single tone of threat, but a people whose history has been flattened by the fear of others.
