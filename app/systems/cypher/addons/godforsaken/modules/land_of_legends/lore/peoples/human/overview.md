# Human Overview

Among the elder races, humans are often dismissed as brief sparks: short-lived, impulsive, and fragile. Yet it is precisely this impermanence that has made them the most adaptable and unpredictable of all peoples.

Where immortals plan in centuries, humans act in generations. They build nations, empires, tribes, city-states, and faiths with an urgency older peoples rarely understand. Their cultures are not uniform, and should never be flattened into one voice.

The great human branches of Land of Legends are shaped by geography, belief, and pressure:

- Caldorans pursue trade, innovation, law, and civic ambition
- Xanthiri bind society to militant faith and disciplined conquest
- Fenmir peoples survive through pride, memory, fracture, and reinvention

Humanity is not a single culture. It is a field of responses to mortality, history, and opportunity.
