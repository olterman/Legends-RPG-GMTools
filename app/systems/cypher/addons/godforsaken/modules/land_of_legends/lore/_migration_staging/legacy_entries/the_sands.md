# **The Sands**
_The Nameless Expanse_
---
## Overview
North of the Fenmir Lowlands and east of Lomêanor lies **The Sands**—a vast, shifting wasteland avoided by all sane peoples.
It is not claimed.
It is not ruled.
it festers.
To cross the Sands is to accept that the world may not recognize you on the other side.
---
## Geography of Unmaking
The Sands are not a single desert, but a fractured region of:
- Ash dunes
- Bone-dust plains
- Black glass fields
- Salt flats that bleed at dawn
Ruins half-buried in sand appear and vanish with the wind. Roads do not remain. Landmarks migrate.
Water exists—but is often poisonous, haunted, or both. The horizon lies.
---
## A Wound, Not a Land
Most scholars agree the Sands were once fertile plains.
They became what they are after the **Alfirin arrival**.
Something went wrong.
The passage through the dimensional aether scarred the land here, thinning reality and allowing **Daelgast resonance** to seep into the world. The Sands are not corrupted. They are **unfinished**.
---
## The Gorothim -_The Terrors_
The **Gorothim** are creatures born of warped flesh, unstable magic, and fractured natural law.
They are:
- Mutated beasts
- Half-formed entities
- Things that remember being something else
They hunt not for hunger, but for **completion**.
Each Gorothim is unique. None breed true.
Slaying one does not make the Sands safer.
---
## The Gurthim -_The Dead_
The **Gurthim** are the restless dead of the Sands.
They do not rise because of necromancy.
They rise because death does not fully occur here.
Bodies decay slowly. Spirits linger. Identity erodes.
Some Gurthim:
- Wander aimlessly
- Guard ruins they do not remember
- Mimic life with terrifying accuracy
Destroying a Gurthim often feels like interrupting a process rather than ending a life.
---
## Daelgast Saturation
Daelgast influence is strongest in the Sands.
Here it manifests as:
- Reality distortion
- Time slippage
- Spontaneous necrosis
- Magical backlash
Even Duath operatives and Duergar scouts avoid prolonged exposure.
The Sands feed the Daelgast—and are fed by it in return.
---
## No Man’s Law
No kingdom claims the Sands.
No faith sanctifies it.
No road crosses it without cost.
Occasionally:
- Cultists attempt pilgrimages
- Warbands vanish
- Scholars overestimate themselves
None establish permanence.
---
## The Edge Settlements
A few desperate or damned groups cling to the fringes:
- Grave-robbers
- Exiles
- Daelgast cult cells
- Doomsday prophets
Their camps are temporary.
Their stories are warnings.
---
## Superstitions and Truths
Common sayings include:
- _“Do not bury your dead in the Sands.”
- _“Do not sleep facing north.”_
- _“If you hear your name, do not answer.”_
All of them are correct.
---
## Why the Sands Matter
The Sands are:
- The visible scar and manifestation of the wound caused by the Alfirin arrival
- The primary Daelgast reservoir
- A natural buffer between Fenmir and Lomêanor
- A narrative pressure zone
If the Sands ever stabilize — something has gone terribly wrong.
---
## A Saying from the Free Cities
> _“Empires fall.
> The Sands remain.”_
