# Secrets

Several truths about the Lands of Legends are not obvious to most inhabitants, even when fragments of them survive in taboo, myth, or forbidden doctrine.

- The world was not merely troubled by strange magic. It was wounded by a failed passage between realities.
- Daelgast is not a scheming evil power. It is corruption without intent, a condition created by unresolved rupture.
- Ul-Nha'rath is distinct from Daelgast. It is the affirmation that collapse is correct, the philosophy that gives persistence and purpose to ruin.
- The gods as commonly imagined are misunderstandings. What mortals call gods are usually pressures, resonances, or interpretive masks placed over larger realities.
- The Old Gods were not destroyed. They were pushed out of alignment, and their distance is part of why the world remains vulnerable.
- The Breath of the World is not a cult mystery or a religion. It is immediacy carried by the living, and it weakens when life yields to despair, domination, or abstraction.

These secrets matter because they change how cults, miracles, corruption, heroism, and history should be understood. The setting becomes far stronger when its deepest truths are not hidden to be shocking, but hidden because most cultures can only bear them in pieces.
