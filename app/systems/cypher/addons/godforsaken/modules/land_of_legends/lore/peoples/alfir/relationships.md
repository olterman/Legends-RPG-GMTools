# Alfir Relationships

The Alfir stand in complicated relationship to every other major force in the setting because their arrival helped shape the world as it is now.

They are bound to the younger peoples through history, influence, resentment, fascination, and unfinished responsibility. Some Alfir branches seek exchange and coexistence. Others prefer stewardship at a distance. The Duathrim seek control rather than partnership.

Their relationship to the world itself is equally conflicted:

- they came as refugees, not natives
- they wounded the world unintentionally through their passage
- they preserve some of the deepest knowledge of what was lost
- they are also among the least able to forget the cost

They maintain different relationships with force and divinity as well. The Alfir do not worship in the ordinary sense. They interpret the cosmos through principles, structures, and consequences. This makes them skeptical of mortal theology, but not immune to ideological rigidity.

Even within Alfir society, relationships remain strained. Shared blood does not erase the fact that sky, forest, deep, and shadow became distinct civilizational answers to trauma.
