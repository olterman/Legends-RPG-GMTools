# The Uruk Tribes
To the common folk of the lowlands and cities, the **Uruk** are little more than monsters given flesh: wild-eyed raiders, feral warbands, and brutal conquerors who respect only strength. Songs and campfire tales rarely grant them history, culture, or reason. In these stories, the Uruk simply _appeared_—riding out of the eastern horizons in a tide of iron, tusks, and blood.
The truth, as with most ancient peoples, is far more complex.
What is known even to Uruk shamans is fragmentary. There are no written chronicles of their earliest days, only oral histories carried in war chants, funeral laments, and the long poems recited beneath open skies. These speak of an eastern homeland now lost, of vast migrations driven by hunger, prophecy, or some forgotten calamity. Whether they fled a greater enemy or followed a vision granted by ancestral spirits remains a matter of debate.
What _is_ certain is this: **one day, the Uruk rode in from the east**, and the world has never regarded them the same since.
---
## The Highland Uruk
High above the cloud line, in the wind-scoured plateaus and deep valleys of the **Fenmir Highlands**, dwell the **Highland Uruk**. To outsiders, their land seems inhospitable—thin air, biting cold, and steep passes that can swallow entire caravans. To the Highland Uruk, it is home, sacred and unyielding.
Despite their fearsome reputation, the Highland Uruk maintain a **reluctant but enduring peace** with the Fenmir Highlanders. This relationship is not born of affection, but of hard-earned respect. Both peoples understand survival in harsh lands, honor in oaths, and the cost of betrayal. Raids between them are rare, and when conflict does arise it is governed by tradition rather than wanton slaughter.
Contrary to popular belief—and even to the expectations set by Uruk martial culture—the Highland Uruk possess **keen philosophical minds**. Among them, warriors are expected not only to fight, but to _think_. They cultivate a rich tradition of **oral philosophy, poetry, and epic song**, often composed and performed after long journeys or successful hunts. These works wrestle with themes of endurance, impermanence, honor, and the tension between strength and wisdom.
Physically, Highland Uruk are unmistakable:
- Skin ranging from pale blue to near-white, weathered by altitude and cold
- Powerful, muscular builds adapted for climbing and long marches
- Prominent lower fangs, often decorated with carvings or metal bands
- Towering stature, even by Uruk standards
- Their way of life is **nomadic**, centered around:
- Seasonal herding of hardy mountain beasts
- Hunting great predators and sky-ranging prey
- Moving clan-camps that follow ancestral routes
To the Highland Uruk, land is not owned—it is _remembered_.

## The Lowland Uruk
If the Highland Uruk complicate the myths, the **Lowland Uruk** confirm them.
Often called **Greenskins** by other races, the Lowland Uruk inhabit fertile plains, river valleys, and forest edges closer to the settled lands. Their society is **openly militant and expansionist**, built upon conquest, dominance, and rigid hierarchy.
Lowland Uruk culture is brutally pragmatic. Strength is law, victory is virtue, and mercy is considered a weakness reserved for the defeated—if at all. Tribes are ruled by war-chiefs who maintain power through constant displays of force, and challenges are settled swiftly and violently.
Beneath the warrior caste exists a **stratified lower class**:
- Farmers who sustain the warbands
- Artisans who forge weapons and armor
- Enslaved peoples taken from conquered tribes and settlements
These slaves are not exclusively non-Uruk; defeated Uruk clans are often absorbed into this lower caste, their identities erased over generations.
It is the Lowland Uruk who have earned the race its grim reputation. Their raids have burned villages, shattered kingdoms, and seeded centuries of fear and hatred. To most of the world, **they are the face of the Uruk**, and few bother to look beyond it.
Physically, Lowland Uruk tend toward:
- Green to dark olive skin tones
- Heavy musculature and thick bone structure
- Shorter, stockier frames than their Highland kin
- Tusks worn blunt from constant combat
Yet even among the Lowland Uruk, there are whispers of deeper traditions—old laws, broken taboos, and prophecies that speak of unity or ruin.
---
## A Divided People
Though Highland and Lowland Uruk share blood, language roots, and ancestral spirits, they view one another with deep suspicion. Highland Uruk see the Lowlanders as having abandoned honor for domination. Lowland Uruk view the Highlanders as weak, indulgent, and dangerously introspective.
Should the Uruk ever unite under a single banner, the consequences would be catastrophic.
For now, they remain divided—misunderstood by the world, and perhaps by themselves.
