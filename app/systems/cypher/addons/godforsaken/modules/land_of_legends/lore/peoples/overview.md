# Peoples Overview

The peoples of the Lands of Legends are not variations on one civilization. They are different answers to the same wounded world.

Some arrived from beyond it. Some were already here. Some adapted faster than anyone expected. Some endured by becoming hidden, mobile, or strange. Taken together, they form the living map of the setting:

- Alfir as immortal exiles divided by how they answered catastrophe
- humans as the most adaptive and politically volatile mortal cultures
- Uruk as a people split between reflective endurance and militant combustion
- Small Folk as hidden, ancient stewards of systems larger peoples ignore
- Fellic as enduring beast-aspected memory bound to the wild
- Lhainim as older-than-civilization attendants of land, threshold, and living pattern
- the Others as peoples whose place in the world is bound to motion, rebellion, or ontological strangeness

No people in the setting exists in isolation. Each is shaped by geography, pressure, myth, and what it believes survival requires.
