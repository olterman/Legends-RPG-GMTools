# **Almadir**
_The Jewel of the Tree_
---
## Overview
South of Cirdion, where the white cliffs give way to rolling green and deepening shadow, lies **Almadir**—the vast forest-realm of the **Galadhrim**, the Tree People.
Almadir is not ruled.
It is **tended**.
To outsiders it appears as a single, endless forest marked in green upon maps. In truth, it is a living mosaic of ancient groves, migrating woodlands, sacred clearings, and regions deliberately left to wild entropy.
Those who enter without invitation rarely leave unchanged.
---
## Origin and Separation
Almadir was founded when a splinter of the Alfirin chose to turn away from stone, council, and harbor.
Where the Kalaquendi of Cirdion sought understanding through exchange, the Galadhrim sought it through **belonging**.
They did not build a city.
They rooted themselves.
The forest accepted them—not as masters, but as **participants**.
---
## The Nature of Almadir
Almadir is alive in a way no other land is.
Trees grow in deliberate patterns. Paths appear and vanish. Clearings migrate over decades. Some groves remember names spoken centuries ago.
Magic here is:
- Instinctive rather than formal
- Responsive rather than predictive
- Protective rather than expansive
Fire behaves strangely. Stone sinks. Metal rusts quickly.
The forest does not forbid intrusion.
It **discourages permanence**.
---
## Galadhrim Society
The Galadhrim live in loose, kin-based enclaves scattered throughout Almadir. These are not settlements in the human sense, but **arrangements**—homes grown rather than built, woven from living wood and leaf.
There is no central authority.
Decisions are made through:
- Grove-councils
- Seasonal gatherings
- Dream-omens interpreted by elders
Leadership is temporary and situational, granted to those most attuned to the forest’s current needs.
---
## The Almadir Covenant
The Galadhrim believe Almadir is not theirs.
They are **sworn to it**.
This covenant binds them to:
- Defend flora and fauna
- Prevent exploitation of forest resources
- Destroy settlements that threaten balance
- Resist fixed borders and permanent roads
This has brought them into conflict with Caldorite loggers, Fenmir settlers, and occasionally even Cirdion envoys.
---
## Relations with Other Realms
**Cirdion:** Respected but distant; stone and root do not easily agree
**Caldor:** Distrusted; progress is seen as a slow violence
**Xanthir:** Open hostility; fire and faith are anathema
**Fellic:** Close allies and kin-spirits
**Velim:** Honored as watchers of balance
**Falthrim:** Known through ancient currents and song
The Galadhrim do not negotiate quickly.
They wait.
---
## The Deep Groves
At Almadir’s heart lie the **Deep Groves**, regions untouched even by Galadhrim feet. These places are older than the Alfirin arrival and respond violently to Daelgast influence.
Some believe the Deep Groves remember the world **before the Scar**.
Others believe they remember something that should never return.
---
## A Saying of Almadir
> _“Stone remembers being broken.
> The tree remembers growing.”_

---
## Why Almadir Matters
Almadir is:
- A living counterbalance to Alfirin hubris
- A Daelgast-resistant biome
- A slow, patient power ignored at great cost
- If Almadir ever moves as one—
the world will feel it.
