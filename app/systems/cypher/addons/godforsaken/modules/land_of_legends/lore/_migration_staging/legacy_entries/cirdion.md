# **Cirdion**
_The First Alfirin Realm_
---
## Overview
**Cirdion** is the oldest Alfirin nation in the Lands of Legends and the first realm established after the arrival from beyond the world. It encompasses the western coasts, high cliffs, sheltered bays, and the surrounding hinterlands claimed by the **Kalaquendi**.
To the Alfirin, Cirdion is not merely a political entity.
It is **the act of arrival made permanent**.
---
## Founding of the Realm
Cirdion was founded at the place where the Alfirin fleet first made landfall after passing through the Gate. What began as a temporary harbor grew into a network of settlements, shipyards, and scholarly enclaves bound by shared purpose rather than borders.
Unlike later Alfirin realms, Cirdion did not arise from separation or secrecy, but from **openness and curiosity**.
Its founding principle was simple:
_If we are to remain, we must understand._
---
## The Capital: **Cird** (also **Cirth**)
The capital city—known as **Cird** in common speech and **Cirth** in formal Kalaquendi pronunciation—is the heart of the realm.
Cird is built upon white stone cliffs overlooking the western sea, arranged in rising terraces that catch light and wind. From a distance, the city appears to float between sea and sky.
Cird is:
- The greatest shipbuilding center in the known world
- The seat of the **High Council of Winds**
- The primary point of contact between Alfirin and other peoples
There are no defensive walls facing the ocean.
Cirdion’s first gaze was westward—and it has never fully turned away.
---
## Governance
Cirdion is ruled by the **High Council of Winds**, a meritocratic body composed of Kalaquendi selected for insight, innovation, and long-term vision.
Merit is measured not by conquest or lineage, but by:
- Contribution to knowledge
- Diplomatic achievement
- Advancement of craft or magic
- Stewardship of interspecies relations
Because the Kalaquendi are immortal, councilors may serve for centuries—or relinquish their role abruptly when they believe their perspective has grown stagnant.
To outsiders, this system appears distant and inscrutable.
To the Kalaquendi, it is simply _patient governance_.
---
## Culture and Society
Cirdion culture emphasizes:
- Dialogue over decree
- Navigation over domination
- Exchange over extraction
Education is universal among Kalaquendi, and even laborers are expected to understand the principles behind their craft.
Art, science, and magic are intertwined; a shipwright may also be a poet, and a diplomat a mathematician.
---
## Relations with Other Realms
**Caldor:** Encouraged but quietly monitored
**Fenmir Free Cities:** Trusted trade partners
**Xanthir:** Formally engaged, privately distrusted
**Galadhrim:** Considered kin, though ideologically distant
**Duathrim:** Publicly denied; privately feared
**Falthrim:** Honored in song and silence
Cirdion sees itself as a bridge between peoples.
Whether it remains strong enough to bear that weight is uncertain.
---
## The Unspoken Knowledge
Within Cird, beneath the public halls, lies the **Hall of Unclosed Charts**—a place where maps of the world, the seas, and other realms remain deliberately unfinished.
Some routes are drawn but never traveled.
Some destinations are named but never discussed.
The Kalaquendi know that arrival was not without consequence.
They simply disagree on whether those consequences can still be undone.
---
## A Saying of Cirdion
> _“We arrived so that the world might be known.”_
