# Uruk Culture

Uruk culture is built around migration, oral memory, strength, and the question of what power is for.

The world often reduces the Uruk to invasion and brutality. That simplification only accurately describes part of them. Highland and Lowland Uruk represent distinct civilizational answers to the same ancestral inheritance.

- Highland Uruk preserve nomadic endurance, oral philosophy, poetry, herding, and restrained honor
- Lowland Uruk preserve war-culture, hierarchy, conquest, and the burning immediacy of collective violence

Both branches value strength, but not in the same way. In the highlands, strength must coexist with thought and memory. In the lowlands, strength becomes proof of right and capacity.

Uruk culture matters because it resists the world's habit of equating fearsome appearance with cultural emptiness.
