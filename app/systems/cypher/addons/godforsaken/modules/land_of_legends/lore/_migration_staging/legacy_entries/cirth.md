# **Cirth**
_The City of First Light, Harbor of the Sky Children_
---
## Overview
**Cirth** is the oldest standing Alfirin city in the Lands of Legends and the first permanent settlement raised after their arrival from beyond the world.
Built upon white stone cliffs overlooking a sheltered western bay, Cirth is both **harbor and monument**—a place where sky, sea, and memory meet.
To the Kalaquendi, it is not merely a city.
It is **proof that arrival was possible**.
---
## Founding and Purpose
Cirth was raised where the Alfirin fleet first made landfall after passing through the Gate. Its name, in old Alfirin, means _“Stone That Floats”_—a reference to the city’s terraces and spires that seem to hover above the cliffs when seen from the sea.
The city was never meant to be a capital.
It was meant to be a **conversation** between peoples and worlds.
Shipwrights, scholars, navigators, and diplomats formed its first councils, believing that understanding this new world would require openness rather than dominion.
---
## Architecture and Appearance
Cirdion is built in layered terraces rising from the harbor to the high councils above.
Key features include:
- Pale stone buildings veined with crystalline light
- Open arches and sky-bridges rather than enclosed streets
- Towers designed to catch wind and light rather than defend
There are no walls facing the sea.
Cirdion does not expect attack from the horizon.
---
## Society and Governance
Cirdion is governed by the **High Council of Winds**, a meritocratic body drawn from scholars, navigators, artisans, and envoys.
Merit is not measured in years or titles, but in **contribution to understanding**:
- New navigational routes
- Diplomatic breakthroughs
- Innovations in magic or craft
- Long-term cultural insight
Because the Kalaquendi are immortal, council positions may be held for centuries—or surrendered abruptly when one feels their insight has grown stale.
To outsiders, the process seems opaque and elitist.
To the Kalaquendi, it is simply _patient_.
---
## The Harbor of First Arrival
Cirdion’s harbor remains one of the finest shipyards in the world.
Alfirin vessels are grown as much as built—wood shaped through song, sailcloth woven with light, hulls balanced to ride both wave and wind.
Human shipbuilders study here, though few are admitted beyond the lower yards.
Some ships have never left the harbor.
Some have never returned.
---
## Relations with the World
**Caldor:** Respected, cautiously encouraged, quietly monitored
**Fenmir Free Cities:** Active trade partners
**Xanthir:** Diplomatic contact only; relations tense
**Galadhrim:** Considered kin, though distant
**Duathrim:** Officially denied existence
**Falthrim:** Acknowledged in song, not in council
Cirdion sees itself as a bridge between peoples.
Whether the bridge still holds is an open question.
---
## The Quiet Truth
Deep within Cirdion lies the **Hall of Unclosed Charts**, where maps of the world—and beyond it—are kept deliberately incomplete.
Some routes are marked but never followed.
Some destinations are named but never explained.
The Kalaquendi know that arrival was not without cost.
They simply disagree on whether the price is still being paid.
