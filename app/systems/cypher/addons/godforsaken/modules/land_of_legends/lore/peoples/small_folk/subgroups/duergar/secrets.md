# Duergar Secrets

The Deep Archives hold more than inconvenient history.

Duergar tradition preserves the idea that some memories, inventions, names, and truths are so destabilizing that they must be entombed rather than transmitted. Officially, this is an act of communal protection. Unofficially, many Duergar fear the Archives are evidence that they are built atop knowledge too dangerous to surface safely.

Their deepest shared dread is expressed not as myth but as structural inevitability: the fear that the stone itself is remembering, and that something buried under enough pressure is not dead, passive, or forgotten.

This secret layer gives Duergar lore its needed unease. They are not just stoic craftsmen underground. They are a people holding themselves together above sealed weight.
