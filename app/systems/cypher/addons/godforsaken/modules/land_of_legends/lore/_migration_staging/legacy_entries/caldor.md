# The Caldor Dominion
_The Age of Tomorrow_
---
## Overview
**Caldor** is the most ambitious human power of the age—an expanding mercantile empire built on innovation, trade, and the belief that the world can be understood, improved, and mastered.
Where Xanthir looks backward to divine absolutes, Caldor looks forward.
And in doing so, it often forgets what lies beneath its foundations.
---
## Governance and Ideology
Caldor is ruled from the city of the same name by a complex web of:
- Merchant guilds
- Academic consortia
- Naval interests
- Elected magistrates
Power is not inherited—it is _earned_, bought, negotiated, or innovated into existence.
The dominant Caldorite belief is **Progress**:
- Knowledge should be shared
- Trade should flow freely
- Old taboos are obstacles, not wisdom
This worldview has brought prosperity… and blind spots.
---
## Territory and Reach
Caldor’s influence spans:
- The central coast
- Major trade routes
- Colonial ports
- A vast island holding to the west
Yet Caldor’s authority grows thinner the farther one travels from its harbors.
---
# The Great Isle of Caldor
_The Unfinished Claim_
---
The **Great Isle** is Caldor’s largest territorial possession—and its greatest contradiction.
Rich in forests, rivers, and mineral wealth, the island remains **largely unexplored**, its interior shielded by near-impenetrable mountain ranges and unpredictable weather.
Official maps show roads and settlements that do not exist.
Unofficial maps show nothing at all.
---
## The Vale
_Where Time Paused_
Hidden within the ring of mountains at the heart of Caldor’s great island lies **the Vale**—a broad, fertile basin centered around a deep, clear lake fed by mountain melt and underground springs.
No roads lead into it.
No ships sail beyond it.
The people of the Vale do not know they are isolated.
They believe the world is simply _this wide_.
---
## Life in the Vale
Life in the Vale follows the lake’s rhythm rather than the sun’s.
Fishing, small-scale farming, herding, and seasonal craft sustain the population. Trade exists only within the Vale; coin is rare and used mainly for ritual exchange or inheritance.
Magic is spoken of cautiously. Old charms exist, but no one remembers when they were first learned.
News travels by foot and boat, not paper.
---
## Major Settlements
### **Lakehome**
A fishing village built along the eastern shore of the great lake.
Lakehome is formed of stilted houses, stone quays, reed docks, and net-lofts weathered smooth by generations of hands. Boats are small, flat-bottomed, and made to glide silently over the water.
Lakehome is known for:
- Skilled fishers and net-makers
- Smokehouses and preserved lake-meat
- Songs sung in rhythm with oars and tides
The elders mark history through **knotted fishing cords**, each pattern representing seasons, floods, and losses.
The lake is treated with reverence—not worship, but respect.
---
### **Mountainhome**
Built into the lower slopes of the surrounding peaks, Mountainhome watches the passes that no one uses.
Its people are herders, stoneworkers, and wardens of ancient trails half-swallowed by rock and pine. They believe the mountains are alive in a slow, deliberate way.
Mountainhome is:
- Insular and cautious
- Rich in old customs
- Deeply suspicious of anything that comes from beyond the ridges
They keep watch, though they do not know what they are watching for.
---
## The Forgotten Choice
No one remembers _why_ the Vale is sealed.
Children are told:
- _“The lake gives, the mountains keep.”_
- _“What crosses the peaks returns changed.”
- _“The world beyond is louder than it should be.”_
Old songs hint that the isolation was **chosen**, not imposed.
Something was refused.
Something else was left outside.
---
## Why the Vale Matters
The Vale is:
- A human culture untouched by empire, faith wars, or Alfirin influence
- A place where Daelgast has never taken hold
- A social blank slate for discovery
- A gentle beginning before a harsh world
For player characters, the Vale is not a starting location.
It is **home**.
And one day, the lake will show something it never has before.
