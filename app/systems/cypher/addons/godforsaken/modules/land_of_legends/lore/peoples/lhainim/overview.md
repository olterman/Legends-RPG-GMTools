# Lhainîm Overview

The Lhainîm gather the smaller, stranger, and more liminal folk whose lives often sit close to hidden water, old growth, folklore, and thresholds other peoples barely notice.

Pixies, Willows, Pucks, Nobs, Redcaps, Dryads, Nayads, and Merfolk should not collapse into one mood, but they do share a place near the edges of ordinary settlement logic. Their stories are often personal, local, uncanny, and bound to place.

As a parent group, `Lhainîm` is especially good for organizing lore that needs to feel:

- folkloric
- uncanny but not always hostile
- tied to hidden ecologies and old agreements
