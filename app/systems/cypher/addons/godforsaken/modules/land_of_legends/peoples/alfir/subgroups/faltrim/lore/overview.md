# Faltrim Overview

The Faltrim should feel like an Alfir branch remembered in fragments, song, and uneasy acknowledgment rather than in clean political certainty.

That makes them especially useful as a lore space for distance, myth, and partial belonging. They are still Alfir, but not comfortably contained by the more visible branches.

Faltrim stories work well when they carry:

- estrangement within kinship
- half-preserved memory
- beauty touched by absence
- the sense that history has not finished deciding what they mean
