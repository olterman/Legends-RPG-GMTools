# Cultural Frame

Cultures in the Lands of Legends are shaped less by destiny than by survival inside a wounded world.

Every major people carries a different answer to the same underlying pressures:

- how to live with the scar left by the Alfirin passage
- how to endure Daelgast without surrendering to it
- how to relate to forces that are real but not personal
- how much change, restraint, movement, or resistance a people can survive

This is why the setting feels old without becoming static. The Alfir preserve memory while fracturing into divergent ways of life. Human societies innovate, adapt, trade, conquer, or endure according to geography and theology. Uruk culture splits between contemplative highland endurance and the burning immediacy of Gor-Kha. Small Folk traditions remain deeply embedded in craft, oral continuity, and hidden stewardship. Fellic, Lhainim, and the Others preserve older rhythms and stranger relationships to land, motion, and identity.

The result is a world where culture is not decorative. It is the main survival technology.
