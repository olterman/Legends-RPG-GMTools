# **The Fenmir Lands**
_The Mortal Heartlands_
---
## Overview
The **Fenmir** were once a single people, bound by shared ancestry and the first human settlements of the central lands. That unity is long gone.
Today, _Fenmir_ no longer names a nation.
It names a **condition**.
Their lands are fractured into highlands, plains, wilds, coasts, and seas—each shaped by survival rather than sovereignty. What unites them is not law or banner, but memory, mortality, and an unyielding refusal to vanish.
---
## The Fenmir Highlands
Rising west and north, the **Fenmir Highlands** are a land of wind-scoured ridges, stone circles, and long memory. Here dwell the **Fenmir Highlanders**—tall, pale-skinned, often marked with ritual tattoos that tell lineage, oath, and spirit-guidance.
They are:
- Nomadic and clan-based
- Deeply spiritual
- Fiercely traditional
The Highlanders coexist with the **Highland Uruk** in an uneasy but enduring balance of respect. Territory is fluid, honor is personal, and violence is restrained by custom rather than treaty.
To the Highlanders, the lowlands are a graveyard.
To the lowlands, the Highlands are a myth that still walks.
---
## The Fenmir Lowlands
Once the cradle of Fenmir civilization, the **Lowlands** are now a scarred expanse of broken fields, abandoned keeps, and half-buried roads.
Repeated Uruk incursions shattered centralized rule here. What remains are:
- Fortified villages
- Wandering warbands
- Mercenary companies
- Ruined cities claimed by ghosts and worse
No single power holds the plains.
Every harvest is a gamble.
---
## The Fenmir Wilds
Encircled by the **Fenmir Mountains**, the Wilds are accessible only through a single western pass. To the east, the land opens into chaos.
The Wilds are:
- Lawless
- Untamed
- Fiercely contested
- They are populated by:
- Lowland Uruk tribes
- Gitz clans
- Scattered human enclaves
- Things that do not belong on maps
The Wilds resist settlement. Roads vanish. Forts fail. Even the Galadhrim watch these lands cautiously.
---
## The Free Cities of the Coast
Along the western and southern coast lie the **Free Cities**—independent city-states born of exile, survival, and opportunity.
The greatest among them:
- **Freeport**
- **Xul**
- **Kahn**
- These cities are bound not by loyalty, but by the **Free Accords**:
- Any race may enter
- Any creed may be practiced
- No slavery within the walls
- No war may be waged inside the harbor
Beyond that, anything is negotiable.
---
## The Treacherous Seas
The waters between:
- **Caldor Island**
- **The Dragon’s Teeth**
- **The Island of Lights**
- **The Bay of Skulls**
…are among the most dangerous in the known world.
These seas are:
- Pirate-infested
- Poorly charted
- Prone to sudden storms and strange currents
Privateers, freebooters, smugglers, and exiles thrive here. Many Free City fortunes are made—or lost—between these waves.
---
## Fenmir Identity
Fenmir culture prizes:
- Adaptability
- Personal honor
- Survival through wit
- Ancestral memory
They are welcoming, suspicious, proud, and pragmatic—often all at once.
Though fractured, Fenmir shamans and ancestral law are still respected across regions, especially in matters of:
- Oath-breaking
- Kin-slaughter
- Spiritual corruption
Even pirates know when not to press their luck.
---
## Why the Fenmir Matter
The Fenmir lands are:
- A crossroads of races and ideologies
- A pressure valve for empires
- A breeding ground for legends
No crown rules here.
Which means **anyone might rise**.
Or fall.
