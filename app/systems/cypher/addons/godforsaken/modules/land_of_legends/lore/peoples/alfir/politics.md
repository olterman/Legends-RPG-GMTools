# Alfir Politics

Alfir politics is dominated by longevity, memory, and the practical consequences of schism.

Among the Kalaquendi, formal power tends toward councils, meritocratic ideals, and the slow accumulation of authority through service, scholarship, and demonstrated judgment. Because Alfir are immortal, however, even a meritocracy can become static, opaque, and exclusionary.

Among the Galadhrim, authority is less bureaucratic and more situational. Leaders emerge from relationship to place, spirit, threat, and communal trust. Power is expected to remain close to the living world rather than hardening into detached administration.

The Faltrim govern through tide-cycles and shifting councils rather than permanent thrones. Political legitimacy rises and falls with movement, migration, and memory carried in song.

The Duathrim represent the most severe Alfir political expression: hierarchical, cell-based, and strategic. Their politics are inseparable from control, secrecy, and long-term manipulation. They do not seek recognition. They seek eventual dominance through invisible positioning.

What makes Alfir politics distinct is that none of these branches have truly forgotten they were once one people. Every Alfir political order therefore carries both internal logic and the ghost of a failed unity.
