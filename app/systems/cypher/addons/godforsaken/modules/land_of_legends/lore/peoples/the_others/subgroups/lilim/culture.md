# Lilim Culture

Lilim culture is mobile, theatrical, and deliberately difficult to pin down.

They gather in caravans, masques, and circles rather than fixed states or blood-bound clans. Those groupings are held together by mutual protection, shared stories, performance, and the understanding that staying still for too long invites both persecution and spiritual stagnation.

Art is not separate from daily life. Masks, costumes, ritual roles, spectacle, and emotional intensity are all part of how Lilim make meaning. Combat, seduction, performance, and ceremony blur together because the Lilim treat expression itself as power.

Their culture teaches movement not just as survival, but as a duty. A Lilim who remains too fixed risks becoming false to the Call that animates them.
