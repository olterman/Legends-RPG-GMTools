# The Fellic
The **Fellic** are an enduring mystery among the peoples of the Lands of Legends. Unlike the great migrations of the Alfirin, the sudden arrivals of other races, or the recorded expansions of humankind, the Fellic have **always been here**.
No myths speak of their arrival.
No chronicles record their beginning.
They are simply part of the wild.
For most of history, the Fellic remained distant observers—rarely hostile, rarely welcoming, moving through forests, plains, and hidden valleys with the same quiet certainty as the seasons. Only in recent ages have Fellic bands begun to engage openly with other peoples, trading, guiding, or occasionally settling near borderlands.
Despite this, they have always maintained close ties with two ancient powers: the **Galadhrim**, whom they regard as kindred spirits of the living wild, and the **Velim**, whom they recognize as wardens of deeper, unseen thresholds.
---
## The Nature of the Fellic
Fellic society does not divide itself by nation or crown, but by **aspect**—animal forms that express spiritual lineage rather than mere biology. Each Fellic lineage carries instincts, traditions, and roles passed through story, dream, and ritual rather than written law.
To the Fellic, civilization is not opposed to nature—it is simply **one of many ecosystems**.
They live by codes of balance, territory, and memory, valuing adaptability and perception over permanence. Their settlements, when they exist at all, are light upon the land and easily abandoned.
---
## The Cat People
The **Cat Fellic** are the most commonly encountered—and the least understood.
Solitary by nature, they are wanderers, scouts, and silent observers. Many serve as intermediaries between the Fellic and the wider world, though always on their own terms. Cat Fellic value independence, curiosity, and personal honor, and they have little patience for rigid hierarchies.
They are often drawn to ancient ruins, abandoned cities, and liminal spaces where history overlaps with the present. Among the Galadhrim, Cat Fellic are respected as watchers and messengers. Among others, they are rumored to be thieves, spies, or worse—accusations they rarely bother to deny.
---
## The Fox People
The **Fox Fellic** are storytellers, negotiators, and tricksters, though the last title is often misunderstood.
Fox Fellic believe that truth is not always served by directness. They trade in symbols, riddles, and layered meanings, and many of their traditions revolve around **social navigation**—how to move through foreign cultures without being consumed by them.
They are the most likely Fellic to integrate with other races, traveling in caravans, serving as diplomats, or embedding themselves within settlements as merchants, entertainers, or advisors. Velim myths suggest the Fox Fellic are unusually sensitive to weakened thresholds, often appearing where reality is thin or change is imminent.
---
## The Dog People
The **Dog Fellic** are defined by loyalty—not to rulers or nations, but to **packs, oaths, and chosen causes**.
They form the most communal of Fellic groups, living in tightly bonded clans that value mutual defense and shared responsibility. Dog Fellic are often found guarding sacred sites, ancient roads, or border regions where conflict is likely.
Though widely assumed to be simple warriors, Dog Fellic possess rich oral traditions, particularly songs and war-chants that encode history, law, and spiritual guidance. Among humans and Alfirin alike, Dog Fellic are respected as steadfast allies—once earned, their loyalty is unbreakable.
---
## The Bear People
The **Bear Fellic** are rare, ancient, and deeply revered among their kind.
They are keepers of memory, strength, and endurance, often serving as elders, judges, or wardens of long-forgotten places. Bear Fellic settlements are few, but their presence is strongly felt in the lands they guard.
They believe that the world moves in great cycles of sleep and waking, growth and collapse. When Bear Fellic stir in numbers, other Fellic know that an age is turning.
The Galadhrim treat Bear Fellic elders as near equals, and Velim traditions acknowledge them as anchors—beings whose sheer presence stabilizes regions where the wild and the real strain against one another.
---
## The Bull Fellic
_The Shapers and the Stayers_
The **Bull Fellic** embody endurance through effort and transformation through labor. Where others adapt by moving or observing, the Bull adapts by **changing the land itself**.
They are builders of terraces, keepers of ancient roads, breakers of cursed soil, and wardens of fertile ground. Bull Fellic are most often found near ancient fields, standing stones, and borders where wilderness meets cultivation.
Unlike many Fellic, Bull Aspects are comfortable with permanence—_earned_ permanence.
They believe that some marks upon the world are acts of care rather than dominance.
Other Fellic rely on Bull elders when deciding whether a place should be reclaimed, reshaped, or left to rest.
---
## The Turtle Fellic
_The Bearers of Time_
The **Turtle Fellic** are rare and seldom seen, even among their own kind.
They carry memory not as story, but as **weight**. Turtle Fellic serve as living archives, remembering cycles that predate kingdoms, climates, and even certain species.
They are most often encountered near coastlines, deep waters, marshlands, or slow rivers—places where land and sea negotiate continuously.
Turtle Fellic teach patience as survival and withdrawal as wisdom. They oppose haste, prophecy, and reactionary change.
Velim records—where they exist—quietly acknowledge Turtle Fellic as stabilizing presences in regions prone to planar stress.
---
## The Bird Fellic
_The Watchers of the High Paths_
The **Bird Fellic** are the messengers, scouts, and dream-walkers of their people.
They live in elevated places—mountain ridges, tall forests, cliff cities, and ruined towers—maintaining watch over the flow of migration, war, and omen.
Bird Fellic are said to see **patterns before events**, not through prophecy, but through perspective. They read winds, stars, smoke, and silence.
They often serve as intermediaries between Fellic communities and the Galadhrim, Velim, or even distant human settlements.
Among the Fellic, when Bird Aspects gather in unusual numbers, it is taken as a sign that **movement is coming**.
---
## The Fellic and the Greater World
The Fellic do not seek dominion, empire, or prophecy. Their concern is **continuity**.
They remember a time before the Alfirin arrival, though they speak of it only obliquely. They remember when the Velim attended quiet Gates, and when forests grew without names. They have endured every age by knowing when to withdraw and when to step forward.
Now, as they begin to engage more openly with the wider world, some wonder if this signals hope—
—or warning.
> _“We do not arrive,”_ say the Fellic.
> _“We notice.”_

## The Myths of the Fellic
_As Remembered, Not Written_
---
## The First Silence
Before names, before paths, before the counting of days, there was the **First Silence**.
In that silence, the world breathed evenly. Forests grew where they wished. Rivers changed their minds. Mountains slept without watchers.
The Fellic say they were born **within** this silence—not created, not summoned, but shaped by the world’s listening.
This is why Fellic elders teach:
> _“We do not speak to the land. We speak **with** it.”_

---
## The Coming of the Bright Wound
The Fellic do not tell of ships or stars.
They tell of a **bright wound in the sky**.
The wound did not bleed light—it _strained it_. The air grew sharp. The ground remembered heat. Animals fled, then returned changed.
The Fellic watched from the edges.
Some say the wound sang.
Some say it screamed.
All agree it did not close.
---
## The Velim and the Narrow Door
When the bright wound appeared, the **Velim** gathered.
Fellic myth says the Velim tried to make the wound smaller, quieter, more careful. They shaped a **narrow door** where a tearing had been.
The Fellic respected this. Narrow doors are survivable.
But the door was held open too long.
The Fellic do not say who held it.
They only say:
> _“The door forgot how to shut.”_

---
## The Alfirin Light
When the **Alfirin** stepped through, the world brightened—and hardened.
Forests grew taller but less patient. Stone remembered weight. Time slowed in some places and raced in others.
The Fellic noticed that the Alfirin did not walk as the world walked.
They endured.
Fellic myths do not judge the Alfirin. Hunger drives all creatures. But they say immortality casts a long shadow, even when it shines.
---
## The Pact of Fur and Leaf
Seeing the change, the Fellic sought allies among those who listened.
The **Galadhrim** heard them first.
Together they made the **Pact of Fur and Leaf**—a promise to guard what still moved freely: beasts, roots, rivers, and the untamed paths between them.
This pact is why Fellic and Galadhrim rarely war, even when they disagree.
> _“The forest remembers who bled for it.”_

---
## The Splitting of Aspects
As the world settled into its wounded shape, the Fellic changed.
Some grew solitary and sharp-eyed.
Some grew clever-tongued and quick-witted.
Some grew steadfast and watchful.
Some grew heavy with memory.
Fellic elders now speak of the **Nine Aspects**, each responding to a different pressure upon the world:
- **Cat** — Observation and independence
- **Fox** — Adaptation and social weaving
- **Dog** — Loyalty and guardianship
- **Bear** — Memory and endurance
- **Bull** — Labor and shaping
- **Turtle** — Time and patience
- **Bird** — Perspective and motion
- (Older myths hint at further Aspects lost to earlier ages.)
Fellic elders say these were not choices.
They were **answers**.
## A Fellic Saying (Expanded)
> _Watch like the Cat._
> _Speak like the Fox._
> _Stand like the Dog._
> _Remember like the Bear._
> _Work like the Bull._
> _Wait like the Turtle._
> _See like the Bird._

---
## The Long Watching
For many ages, the Fellic withdrew.
They became shadows in tall grass, rumors in market towns, half-glimpsed eyes at the edge of roads. They watched empires rise and fall, forests burn and regrow, Gates open and seal.
They waited for the world to decide what it would become.
---
## The Stirring (The Present Age)
Now the Fellic say the Silence is thinning again.
The bright wound aches.
The narrow doors creak.
The Velim move more often.
The Galadhrim listen harder.
And so the Fellic step forward once more—not as rulers, not as prophets, but as witnesses.
> _“When we stop watching,”_ say the Bear elders,
> _“something ends.”_
