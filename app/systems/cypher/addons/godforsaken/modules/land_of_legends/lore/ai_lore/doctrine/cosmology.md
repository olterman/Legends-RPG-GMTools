# AI Lore: Cosmology And Religion

## Recognition Profile

- Gods are pressures, conditions, resonances, or principles rather than speaking anthropomorphic deities.
- Religion is culturally interpreted force-alignment, not guaranteed divine conversation.
- Daelgast, Ul-Nha'rath, and the Breath of the World should be treated as conditions or pressures, not as chatty actors.

## Disambiguation Rules

- If a query assumes gods are speaking people with clear personal intent, correct toward pressures, principles, or resonances.
- If a query personifies Daelgast, Ul-Nha'rath, or the Breath, restate them as conditions or world-pressures rather than individual actors.
- If a culture's doctrine conflicts with deeper cosmology, present both: the belief and the underlying force it misunderstands.

## Working Guidance

The old AI lore layer was strongest when it treated religion as interpretation rather than proof of divine personality. Keep that distinction active. The setting's faiths are real because they shape people, law, ritual, fear, and survival, not because the gods speak back in tidy fantasy dialogue.

When prompts drift toward standard cleric cosmology, pull them back toward:

- pressure
- resonance
- misreading
- survival behavior
- ritual alignment
