# **Ered Engrin  & The Duergar**
_The Deepwardens of Ered Engrin_
---
## Overview
The **Duergar** dwell beneath the **Ered Engrin**, the Iron Mountains that sever the southern forests of Almadir from the lands beyond. They are not conquerors, nor recluses by fear, but **keepers of depth**—a people shaped by pressure, silence, and endurance.
Where others see the mountains as a barrier, the Duergar know them as a **burden that must be borne**.
They do not seek the surface, nor do they deny it.
They simply understand what lies beneath it.
---
## Origins and Memory
Duergar oral history claims their people were **called downward** long before the arrival of the Alfirin. Whether by instinct, warning, or fate, they delved into the roots of the world and remained.
Alfirin records suggest the Duergar **sealed themselves away** during the Gatefall, locking ancient passages and stabilizing fractures caused by the crossing.
The Duergar neither confirm nor deny this.
For them, memory is less important than **continuity**.
---
## The Holds of Iron
Duergar civilization exists entirely within the mountain.
Each **Hold** is a vertical world of:
- Forge-halls
- Ancestor-vaults
- Resonance chambers
- Deepward sanctums
Holds are connected by ancient tunnelways that follow stress-lines in the stone rather than geography. Many routes are collapsed by design.
Expansion is not a goal.
**Stability is.**
---
## Society and Order
Duergar society is structured around **Purpose**, not rank.
Every Duergar belongs to:
- A **Forge-Clan** (craft and lineage)
- A **Depth-Circle** (responsibility within the Hold)
- Leadership is granted to those who:
- Endure longest
- Listen most
- Speak least
Art exists as form, pattern, and resonance—carvings meant to strengthen stone, songs meant to calm pressure, symbols meant to remind.
Beauty is not decoration.
It is reinforcement.
---
## The Stonebound Faith
The Duergar do not worship gods in the surface sense.
They revere:
- Weight
- Balance
- Containment
Their rites are acts of **maintenance**, performed to prevent collapse—both physical and metaphysical.
Some scholars believe these rituals anchor reality where it thins.
The Duergar do not correct them.
---
## Relations with Almadir
Between the Duergar and the Galadhrim exists a **silent accord**.
The forest does not press into the deep
The mountain does not rise into the green
Contact is rare and purposeful, occurring only when imbalance threatens both domains.
Neither people claims ownership.
Both accept responsibility.
---
## Relations with Other Peoples
**Cirdion:** Distant trade, carefully regulated
**Caldor:** Limited cooperation; surface urgency is often mistrusted
**Xanthir:** Wary tolerance; fire and doctrine are unstable forces
**Velim:** Closely watched, sometimes aided
**Fellic:** Recognized as surface-echoes of older patterns
**Daelgast:** Actively contained wherever possible
The Duergar do not explain their actions.
They assume survival will justify them in time.
---
## The Deep Warnings
There are places beneath Ered Engrin where:
- Stone hums without vibration
- Metal corrodes without heat
- Sound travels in circles
These regions are sealed.
Marked.
Never spoken of.
Duergar children are taught that some depths are not meant to be understood—only **held shut**.
---
## A Saying of the Duergar
> _“We do not hold the mountain.
> We hold what the mountain keeps from rising.”_

---
## Why the Duergar Matter
The Duergar are:
- A stabilizing force in a damaged world
- A counterbalance to Alfirin arrival
- A quiet resistance to Daelgast spread
- If they ever abandon their vigil, it will not be from defeat—
but from exhaustion.
