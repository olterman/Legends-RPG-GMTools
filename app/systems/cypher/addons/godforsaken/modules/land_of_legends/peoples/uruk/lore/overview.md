# Uruk Overview

The Uruk are powerful, territorial, and culturally deep-rooted, not merely a hostile mass for frontier stories.

Their branches matter. Highland Uruk and Lowland Uruk share ancestry and force of presence, but they do not occupy the same land, face the same pressures, or produce the same social shape. Treating `Uruk` as a top-level people is useful, but only if the subgroup level carries the real distinctions.

In play, Uruk lore works best when it avoids reducing them to a single tone of violence and instead makes room for:

- local custom
- clan identity
- coexistence and rivalry
- memory, territory, and practical survival
