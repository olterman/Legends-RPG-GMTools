# Duergar Culture

Duergar culture is organized around pressure, endurance, oath, and craft.

Their societies are built through Holds: subterranean complexes that are simultaneously fortress, forge, archive, shrine, and civic machine. These are not merely settlements. They are proofs of survival under weight.

Creation is sacred to the Duergar, but never casual. Their craft tradition values endurance, function, resonance, and structural meaning over ornament or display. Innovation is accepted only after repeated failure-testing, because a thing that has not met strain has not earned trust.

Even their identity is oath-shaped. A Duergar belongs through Stone-Oaths carried between guilds, families, and roles. To be Duergar is to be bound, tested, and answerable.
